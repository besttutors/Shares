Q 1 Find the dividend received on 60 shares of ₹20 each if 9% dividend is declared.
Concept
Dividend is always calculated on Face Value (Nominal Value), not market price.
  • Dividend on 1 share = 9% of ₹20 = (9/100) × 20 = ₹1.80
  • Dividend on 60 shares = 60 × ₹1.80
Annual Dividend = ₹108
Q 2 A company declares 8% dividend. A man receives ₹2840 as dividend. Find the nominal value of his shares.
Concept
Dividend% is applied on Nominal Value. So: 8% × NV = ₹2840. Solve for NV.
  • Let Nominal Value = ₹x
  • (8/100) × x = 2840
  • x = 2840 × 100 ÷ 8 = ₹35,500
Nominal Value of shares = ₹35,500
Q 3 A man buys 250, ten-rupee shares each at ₹12.50. Rate of dividend = 7%. Find (a) annual dividend, (b) % return on investment.

(a) Annual Dividend

Concept
Dividend uses Face Value (₹10), not market price. Investment uses Market Value (₹12.50).
  • FV = ₹10, MV = ₹12.50, Shares = 250
  • Total Face Value = 250 × 10 = ₹2,500
  • Dividend = 7% of ₹2,500 = ₹175
Annual Dividend = ₹175

(b) % Return

Concept
% Return = (Income ÷ Actual Money Invested) × 100. Investment = Shares × MV.
  • Investment = 250 × ₹12.50 = ₹3,125
  • % Return = (175 ÷ 3125) × 100 = 5.6%
% Return = 5.6%
Q 4 Find the market price of 5% ₹100 share when a person gets ₹65 dividend by investing ₹1430.
Concept
Find no. of shares from dividend formula, then Market Price = Total Investment ÷ No. of Shares.
  • Let shares = x  ⟹  x × 5% × 100 = 65  ⟹  5x = 65  ⟹  x = 13
  • Market Price = 1430 ÷ 13 = ₹110
Market Price per share = ₹110
Q 5 Govind buys 50 shares of FV ₹100 at ₹132. Dividend = 7.5% p.a. (i) Investment? (ii) Annual income? (iii) Extra shares for ₹150 more income?

(i) Investment

Concept
Investment is at Market Value (the actual price paid).
  • Investment = 50 × ₹132 = ₹6,600
Investment = ₹6,600

(ii) Annual Income

  • Income = 50 × 7.5% × 100 = ₹375
Annual Income = ₹375

(iii) Extra Shares for ₹150 more

Concept
Find total shares needed for new income (375+150 = ₹525), then subtract existing 50 shares.
  • New income = ₹375 + ₹150 = ₹525
  • New shares x: x × 7.5% × 100 = 525  ⟹  7.5x = 525  ⟹  x = 70
  • Extra shares = 70 − 50 = 20
Extra shares to buy = 20
Q 6 A lady holds 1800, ₹100 shares paying 15% dividend. Find annual dividend. Bought at 40% premium, find % return.
Concept
At 40% premium, MV = 100 + 40 = ₹140. % Return uses total investment at MV.
  • Annual Dividend = 1800 × 15% × 100 = ₹27,000
  • MV per share = ₹100 + 40% of ₹100 = ₹140
  • Total Investment = 1800 × 140 = ₹2,52,000
  • % Return = (27000 ÷ 252000) × 100 = 75/7 = 10 5/7 %
Annual Dividend = ₹27,000  |  % Return = 10⁵⁄₇ %
Q 7 What sum to invest in ₹25 shares at ₹36, to earn ₹720 income? Dividend = 12%. Also find % return.
Concept
Find shares from dividend formula, then Investment = Shares × MV. % Return = Income ÷ Investment × 100.
  • x × 12% × 25 = 720  ⟹  3x = 720  ⟹  x = 240 shares
  • Investment = 240 × ₹36 = ₹8,640
  • % Return = (720 ÷ 8640) × 100 = 25/3 = 8⅓%
Sum to invest = ₹8,640  |  % Return = 8⅓%

Q 21 Two companies: 7% shares at ₹116 and 9% shares at ₹145. Which is more profitable? (FV = ₹100)
Concept
Compare income per ₹1 invested. Higher ratio = more profitable investment.
  • Company 1: Income on ₹116 = 7  →  Income per ₹1 = 7/116 = 35/580
  • Company 2: Income on ₹145 = 9  →  Income per ₹1 = 9/145 = 36/580
  • 36 > 35  →  Company 2 is more profitable
9% shares at ₹145 are more profitable
Q 22 Which is better: 6% ₹100 shares at ₹120 OR 8% ₹10 shares at ₹15?
Concept
Compare income per ₹1 invested. The LCM method makes fractions comparable.
  • Option 1: Income on ₹120 = 6% of 100 = ₹6  →  per ₹1 = 6/120 = 1/20 = 15/300
  • Option 2: Income on ₹15 = 8% of 10 = ₹0.80  →  per ₹1 = 0.8/15 = 4/75 = 16/300
  • 16 > 15  →  Option 2 is better
8% ₹10 shares at ₹15 is the better investment

Q 31 Man invests ₹6750 partly in 6% shares at ₹140 and partly in 5% shares at ₹125. Total income = ₹280. Find each investment.
Concept
Income per ₹1 invested = Dividend per share ÷ MV. Set up equation with total income = ₹280.
  • Let investment in 6% shares = ₹x  →  other = ₹(6750 − x)
  • Income on ₹x at 6%/₹140: (6/140)x = (3/70)x
  • Income on ₹(6750−x) at 5%/₹125: (5/125)(6750−x) = (1/25)(6750−x)
  • (3/70)x + (1/25)(6750−x) = 280
  • Multiply by 350: 15x + 14(6750−x) = 98000
  • 15x + 94500 − 14x = 98000  ⟹  x = 3500
  • Other part = 6750 − 3500 = ₹3,250
In 6% shares = ₹3,500  |  In 5% shares = ₹3,250

A-RAssertion-Reason Questions

A-R 1 A: Dividend depends on market value.  R: Dividend is always % of face value.
Analysis
  • Assertion (A) is FALSE — Dividend does NOT depend on market value at all.
  • Reason (R) is TRUE — Dividend is always a % of Face Value (Nominal Value), regardless of market price.
  • Example: 10% dividend on ₹100 FV = ₹10, whether MV is ₹80 or ₹150.
Option (B): A is false, R is true
A-R 2 Quotation: "10% ₹120 shares at ₹1200". A: Company is in profit.  R: Share is at premium.
Analysis
  • FV = ₹120, MV = ₹1200 → MV > FV → Share IS at premium ✓ → R is TRUE
  • Company paying 10% dividend → it is making profit → A is TRUE
  • But: premium (R) is not the reason the company is in profit (A). Company profit depends on its earnings, not share price.
Option (D): Both A and R are true, but R is NOT the correct reason for A
A-R 3 1200 shares, FV ₹50, discount ₹10. A: Total investment = ₹60,000.  R: MV of each share = ₹40.
Analysis
  • MV = 50 − 10 = ₹40 → R is TRUE ✓
  • Investment = 1200 × 40 = ₹48,000 ≠ ₹60,000 → A is FALSE ✗
  • (₹60,000 would be at face value; we must use MV = ₹40)
Option (B): A is false, R is true

CT 6 Man has x shares of 6% ₹100 at par. Sells half at 10% discount, invests in 7% ₹50 shares at ₹10 premium. Income decreases by ₹120. Find (i) original shares, (ii) shares sold, (iii) initial income.
Concept
New income = income from remaining half (old) + income from new shares. Old income − New income = 120.
  • Selling price per old share = 100 − 10 = ₹90  →  Proceeds = 90 × x/2 = 45x
  • New shares MV = 50 + 10 = ₹60  →  New shares = 45x ÷ 60 = 3x/4
  • Old full income = x × 6% × 100 = 6x
  • New total income = (x/2)×6% ×100 + (3x/4)×7%×50 = 3x + 21x/8 = 45x/8
  • 6x − 45x/8 = 120  ⟹  3x/8 = 120  ⟹  x = 320
Original shares = 320  |  Shares sold = 160  |  Initial Income = ₹1,920

CT 8 Man buys ₹40 shares, 10% dividend. He gets 16% profit on investment. At what price did he buy?
Concept
16% return on MV = dividend per share. So: 16% × MV = 10% × FV. Solve for MV.
  • Dividend = 10% of 40 = ₹4
  • 16 = (4 ÷ MV) × 100  ⟹  MV = 400 ÷ 16 = ₹25
Purchase price per share = ₹25

Calculate Virat's dividend, set equal to Dhoni's (with unknown rate r%), solve for r.
